Ad Exposure Across Social Platforms

Introduction

This data story explores how many ads people are exposed to during short sessions on social media. The data was collected through self-logged sessions where users recorded which platforms they used and how many ads they encountered in a 5-minute window.

We analyze how ad exposure changes based on time of day, platform used, and whether the user engaged with the ad.

Data Source

The data set was collected using a Google Form filled out by volunteers. Each row represents one 5-minute session of social media use, and includes:

  • Timestamp
  • Platforms used (e.g., TikTok, Instagram)
  • Number of ads seen
  • Relevance of ads
  • User engagement

Data cleaning and wrangling were done using the tidyverse, lubridate, and stringr packages in R.

Ads Seen by Platform

A comprehensive boxplot analysis depicting the number of ads viewed in 5 minutes across various social media platforms: Facebook, Instagram, TikTok, YouTube, X, and the all-encompassing ‘all of the above’. The median ad count is most pronounced for ‘Facebook users, indicating a significant ad exposure when using multiple platforms. When examined individually, platforms like Instagram, TikTok, and X show similar medians, with TikTok and Instagram displaying a broader range. Facebook and YouTube, however, demonstrate lower ad exposure on average. For users who select ’all of the above’, the platform boasts the highest median and the widest spread of ads seen due to the combined exposure.

Key features:

The platform “all of the above” has the highest median and widest spread of ads seen.

Platforms like Instagram, TikTok, and X have similar medians, but TikTok and Instagram have wider variability.

Facebook and YouTube show lower ad exposure on average compared to “all of the above”. ## Summary

Our findings suggest that:

  • Time of day matters – more ads are seen during the evening
  • Platform matters – TikTok and Instagram users see more ads
  • Multi-platform usage increases exposure – users using multiple apps report higher ad counts

This analysis sheds light on the digital advertising environment and its relationship with social behavior on platforms.
